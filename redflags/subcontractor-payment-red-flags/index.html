5 Subcontractor Payment Red Flags

Veteran subs know these signs. When you see them, start documenting everything. Your daily reports are about to become the most valuable asset you own.

1. Verbal change orders with no paperwork. "Just do it, we'll sort out the change order later." Document the verbal instruction in your daily report — date, time, who said it, what they authorized.
2. Site super "unavailable" for sign-off. The person who approves your work is suddenly hard to find. VoiceLogPro's geotagged reports prove you were on site and completed the work regardless.
3. "We'll catch you up on the next draw." The most expensive five words in construction. Every time you hear this, increase your documentation frequency. Daily reports with photos protect your lien rights.
4. New PM who "needs to review everything." New project manager questioning previously approved work is positioning to renegotiate. Your historical daily reports prove the original approvals.
5. GC asking you to keep working past your contract scope. Scope creep without a change order is unpaid work. Log every out-of-scope task in your daily report and send written confirmation the same day.
