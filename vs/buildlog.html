VoiceLogPro vs BuildLog

Short answer: pick VoiceLogPro if you're a solo subcontractor or small crew who wants a court-ready PDF from a voice note for a flat $49/month. Pick BuildLog if you're a general contractor or claims team documenting delay across multiple sites and need its claim-tagging taxonomy and per-site team management.

These two tools are more alike than most comparisons admit. Both are voice-first: you talk while you walk the site, and the app turns your words into a timestamped daily report. Both attach GPS-tagged photos, pull in weather, work offline, and lock the record so it holds up later. So the real question isn't "which one has voice" — it's how much platform you need around the voice note, and what you want to pay for it.

What BuildLog does well

BuildLog is built for the claim. Its whole design assumes you may end up in a delay dispute and need an evidence package a lawyer will respect — "if it didn't happen in your daily log, it didn't happen."

  • Claim-Relevant Tagging — flag each report with delay categories (weather, equipment, owner-directed, permitting, RFI, differing conditions, force majeure) so a pattern is provable later
  • Delay Defense Pack — generates structured PDFs with timelines, weather summaries, photos, and evidence-integrity blocks
  • Tamper-evident by design — SHA-256 hashes, GPS, and version history lock content after submission
  • Multi-site, multi-user — organize sites and teams under one account, up to unlimited on Enterprise

Where VoiceLogPro fits better

VoiceLogPro strips the same core idea — voice note in, defensible PDF out — down to one person and one flat price. There's nothing to configure and no seats to provision.

  • One operator, zero setup — open the app, speak, get a PDF. No tagging taxonomy to learn, no sites to organize
  • Flat pricing — free Defense Kit, then a single $49/month Crew Plan instead of per-site tiers that climb to $799
  • No lock-in — reports export as standard PDFs; leave anytime and keep everything you generated
  • Built for the trades — framed around payment disputes, lien waivers, and schedule-compression claims a subcontractor actually faces
Honest note: if you manage several crews across multiple jobsites and expect to litigate delay claims, BuildLog's tagging and per-site structure earn their price. VoiceLogPro deliberately doesn't do multi-site team management — that simplicity is the point, not an oversight.

When to choose each

Choose VoiceLogPro if…

  • You're a subcontractor or single foreman, not running a multi-site program
  • You want the lowest fixed cost and no per-user math
  • You mainly need a clean, timestamped PDF to defend against a payment or back-charge dispute

Choose BuildLog if…

  • You coordinate several sites or crews under one account
  • You want delay-category tagging to build a provable pattern for a claim
  • Your claims team needs structured Delay Defense Pack exports across projects

Our honest verdict

BuildLog is the heavier, team-scale delay-claims platform — worth it for GCs and claims departments. VoiceLogPro is the fastest, cheapest way for a single subcontractor to turn a spoken note into a court-ready PDF. Same core idea; different weight class.

Frequently asked questions

Is VoiceLogPro a BuildLog alternative?

Yes, for solo subcontractors and small crews. Both convert voice notes into tamper-evident daily-report PDFs. VoiceLogPro charges one flat rate and keeps the workflow to a single operator; BuildLog prices per site and adds multi-team claim-tagging tools that larger GCs and claims departments need.

Are both voice-first?

Yes — both let you dictate a report from the jobsite instead of typing. The difference is scope: BuildLog wraps voice capture in a multi-site claims platform with a delay-category tagging taxonomy, while VoiceLogPro keeps it to speak-then-PDF for one person.

Which is cheaper?

VoiceLogPro is a flat $49/month Crew Plan with a free Defense Kit tier. BuildLog's published plans start at $99/month (3 sites, 5 users) and scale to $299 and $799/month. For a single subcontractor, VoiceLogPro is lower-cost; for a multi-site GC, BuildLog's per-site model may pay off.
