Step-by-Step Guide

1. Compile all daily reports chronologically

Organize daily reports from project start to completion. The owner will need these for warranty claims, insurance purposes, and future renovations.

2. Prepare the closeout binder

Include: permit documents, inspection certificates, warranties, equipment manuals, as-built drawings, lien releases, and the complete daily report archive.

3. Conduct the final walkthrough

Walk the project with the owner, noting any punch list items. Document the walkthrough with photos and have both parties sign the completion certificate.

4. Transfer warranties and guarantees

Provide all manufacturer warranties (roofing, HVAC, plumbing, electrical) with start dates aligned to substantial completion.

5. Submit final payment documentation

Include final pay applications, lien releases from all subcontractors, and the certificate of substantial completion.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I retain project handover documents?

Retain for the full warranty period (typically 1-2 years) plus the statute of limitations for construction defects (varies by state, 4-12 years). Digital archives are standard.

Do daily reports need to be included in handover?

Yes. Daily reports are part of the project record and should be included in digital format. Owners may need them for future claims, disputes, or renovations.

What is a certificate of substantial completion?

A document signed by the architect or engineer certifying that the project is sufficiently complete for the owner to occupy or use. It starts the warranty clock and triggers final payment.
