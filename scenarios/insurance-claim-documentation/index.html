Insurance Claim Documentation: How Daily Reports Support Property Damage Claims

Real scenario: $180K storm damage claim approved in 11 days using VoiceLogPro daily reports as primary evidence.

A construction site suffered $180,000 in storm damage to materials and partially completed work. The insurance company initially offered $95,000. Daily reports closed the gap.

The Scenario

A severe thunderstorm with 70 mph winds hit a residential development in Texas. Pre-installed siding was torn off, stored lumber was waterlogged, and a partially framed structure collapsed. The insurance adjuster initially estimated $95,000 in damages.

The Evidence

The GC's VoiceLogPro daily reports from the 30 days before the storm documented:

  • Exact materials on-site with quantities and costs (stored lumber: 240 2x10x16, 180 sheets of 3/4 plywood)
  • Work completed and in progress (siding: 60% installed on units 3-7)
  • Pre-storm condition of all structures (framing status by unit)
  • Photographs of materials and progress (timestamped)

The day after the storm, the GC produced a VoiceLogPro damage assessment report documenting:

  • Specific damage by unit and material type
  • Quantities of damaged materials (240 boards waterlogged, 1,200 sq ft siding torn off)
  • Structural damage assessment (unit 4 framing collapsed, units 5-7 intact)
  • Cleanup labor hours and equipment needed

The Outcome

Result: Insurance company increased the settlement to $172,000 (96% of the claimed amount) within 11 days. The adjuster specifically cited the 'exceptional quality of contemporaneous documentation' as the reason for the quick resolution.

Frequently Asked Questions

What do insurance adjusters look for in daily reports?

Adjusters want: (1) pre-loss condition documentation, (2) specific material quantities and values, (3) timestamped photos, (4) post-loss damage assessment with the same specificity. The closer your daily reports match these needs, the faster and larger your settlement.

Should I document materials on-site even if not installed?

Yes. Stored materials are insurable but only if documented. Your daily report should list delivered materials by type, quantity, and storage location. Undocumented materials are difficult to claim.

How quickly should I document damage after an event?

Within 24 hours. The same-day damage report is the most powerful evidence. Insurance companies view reports made days later with skepticism. VoiceLogPro lets you document damage by voice while walking the site.
