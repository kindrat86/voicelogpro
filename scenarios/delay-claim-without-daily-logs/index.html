Scenario: Delay Claim Without Daily Logs — The $250,000 Lesson

A concrete subcontractor on a Virginia data center project loses 18 days to weather. The GC assesses $250,000 in liquidated damages. The subcontractor files a delay claim — and loses because they can't prove which days were weather-impacted.

What went wrong: The subcontractor had no daily records. They remembered "it rained a lot in March" but couldn't say which specific days were lost, couldn't prove crews were ready to work on those days, and couldn't document mitigation efforts. The GC's records showed "no work performed" on those dates with no explanation. The claim was denied in arbitration. Lesson: weather data + crew logs = delay claim evidence. Memory = nothing.
