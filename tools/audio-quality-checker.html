    Frequently Asked Questions

    What sample rate do I need for accurate transcription?

    16 kHz is the minimum most speech-to-text engines accept (Whisper, Deepgram, Google). 44.1 kHz or 48 kHz is ideal and matches what phones and recorders produce by default. Anything below 16 kHz will lose high-frequency consonant sounds and hurt accuracy.

    How does background noise affect transcription?

    Speech-to-text accuracy drops sharply once signal-to-noise ratio falls below ~15 dB. A café, an active job site, or a car at highway speed all sit around 5–10 dB SNR and typically cause 10–20% more errors. Move to a quieter spot, use a directional mic, or hold the phone closer.

    Why was my file flagged for duration?

    Most APIs cap single requests at 1–4 hours depending on provider. Very short clips (under ~1 second) often fail voice-activity detection. For daily logs, aim for 30 seconds to 30 minutes per clip — the sweet spot for both accuracy and cost.
