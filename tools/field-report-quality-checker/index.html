What affects transcription quality?

Sample Rate: 16 kHz minimum for good ASR. 8 kHz (phone quality) loses high-frequency consonants causing word errors. 44.1 kHz+ captures full frequency range.

Background Noise: Even moderate construction site noise (tools, wind, traffic) reduces accuracy by 15–30%. Use noise-cancelling or close-mic techniques.

Microphone Type: Lavalier or headset mics capture clear speech at the source, rejecting ambient noise. Built-in mics pick up full room reverb.

Speaker Count: Solo speakers transcribe best. Each additional speaker adds crosstalk and diarization errors, reducing per-speaker accuracy.

Duration: Shorter clips (under 30 min) have lower risk of drift. Longer recordings benefit from periodic restarts or chapter markers.
