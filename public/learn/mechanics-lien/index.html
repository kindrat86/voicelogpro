What Is a Mechanics Lien?

Complete guide to mechanics liens: what they are, how they work, and deadlines.

A mechanics lien is a legal claim against a property for unpaid construction work. It is one of the most powerful payment protection tools available to contractors.

How Mechanics Liens Work

When a contractor performs work on a property and is not paid, they can file a lien against the property. This lien must be resolved before the property can be sold or refinanced.

Key Deadlines

Every state has different lien deadlines, ranging from 30 days to 12 months. Some states also require preliminary notices within 15-30 days of starting work. Missing these deadlines eliminates your lien rights.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a preliminary notice?

A preliminary notice (also called Notice to Owner or pre-lien notice) is a document sent to the property owner informing them that you are working on the project and may file a lien if not paid. Required in most states.
