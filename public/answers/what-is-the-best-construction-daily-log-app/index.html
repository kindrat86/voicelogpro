How to choose based on your trade

Electricians, plumbers, HVAC, roofers (subcontractors): You need fast, defensible daily documentation — not project budgeting. VoiceLogPro is purpose-built for this: speak your log, get a PDF. No project management overhead, no training required, $19/month. The court-admissible PDF output (timestamped, geotagged, weather data included) directly protects mechanic's lien and delay claim rights — the reason you're keeping logs in the first place.

General contractors running $2M+ projects: You need daily logs integrated with RFIs, submittals, budgets, and schedules. Procore's daily log module is part of a larger platform — you're paying for the integration, not just the log. The trade-off: higher cost, steeper learning curve, and daily log entry that takes longer (typing, not voice). Best when logs are one piece of a larger project management workflow.

Mid-size crews needing photos + logs: Raken and Fieldwire sit between VoiceLogPro and Procore. Raken excels at photo-heavy field reports with time cards; Fieldwire adds plan markup and punch lists. Both require typing and cost more than VoiceLogPro's voice-first approach.

What matters most for daily logs specifically

When evaluating daily log apps, prioritize these four factors — they're what make the difference between a log that protects you and one that's just digital paperwork:

  1. Speed of entry: Voice is 4x faster than typing on a jobsite. If logging takes more than 3 minutes, foremen skip it or backfill generic entries — defeating the legal purpose.
  2. Court admissibility: The log must be contemporaneous (timestamped at creation), corroborated (weather data, geotagged photos), and consistent (same format daily). Apps that allow backdating or editing timestamps undermine admissibility.
  3. Trade-specific fields: An electrician's daily log needs different fields than a roofer's. Look for apps that support trade-specific templates rather than one-size-fits-all.
  4. Integration with existing tools: If you already use Procore or BuilderTrend for project management, an integrated daily log module may save duplicate entry. But if you just need logs, a standalone tool avoids platform lock-in and unnecessary cost.
