Florida Mechanics Lien Law Guide for Subcontractors

TL;DR: In Florida, the preliminary notice rule is: Notice to Owner within 45 days. You must file your lien within 90 days from last furnishing, and the enforcement deadline is 1 year. Daily construction reports are critical evidence for every step.

Preliminary Notice Requirements

In Florida, subcontractors must follow strict notice rules to preserve lien rights. The key requirement: Notice to Owner within 45 days. Missing this deadline forfeits your lien rights entirely — no exceptions. Voice Log Pro's voice-to-PDF daily reports timestamp every notice delivery automatically, creating an audit trail that holds up in court.

Lien Filing Deadline

After your last day of work or material delivery, you have a window of 90 days from last furnishing to file your mechanics lien. This deadline is absolute. Late filing = no lien. Your daily reports serve as the definitive record of when work was performed, eliminating disputes about the last-furnishing date that GCs and owners commonly exploit.

Enforcement Period

Once filed, your lien is valid for 1 year. If payment is not received, you must initiate foreclosure proceedings within this window. Without contemporaneous daily documentation, proving your case in court becomes your word against theirs.
