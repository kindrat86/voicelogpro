Colorado Mechanics Lien Law Guide for Subcontractors

TL;DR: In Colorado, the preliminary notice rule is: Notice of Intent within 10 days. You must file your lien within 4 months from last work, and the enforcement deadline is 6 months. Daily construction reports are critical evidence for every step.

Preliminary Notice Requirements

In Colorado, subcontractors must follow strict notice rules to preserve lien rights. The key requirement: Notice of Intent within 10 days. Missing this deadline forfeits your lien rights entirely — no exceptions. Lien Filing Deadline

After your last day of work or material delivery, you have a window of 4 months from last work to file your mechanics lien. This deadline is absolute. Late filing = no lien. Your daily reports serve as the definitive record of when work was performed, eliminating disputes about the last-furnishing date that GCs and owners commonly exploit.

Enforcement Period

Once filed, your lien is valid for 6 months. If payment is not received, you must initiate foreclosure proceedings within this window. Without contemporaneous daily documentation, proving your case in court becomes your word against theirs.
