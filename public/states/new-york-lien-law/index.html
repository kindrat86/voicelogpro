New York Mechanics Lien Law Guide for Subcontractors

TL;DR: In New York, the preliminary notice rule is: no preliminary notice (private). You must file your lien within 8 months (single-family) / 4 months (commercial), and the enforcement deadline is 1 year. Daily construction reports are critical evidence for every step.

Preliminary Notice Requirements

In New York, subcontractors must follow strict notice rules to preserve lien rights. The key requirement: no preliminary notice (private). Missing this deadline forfeits your lien rights entirely — no exceptions. Lien Filing Deadline

After your last day of work or material delivery, you have a window of 8 months (single-family) / 4 months (commercial) to file your mechanics lien. This deadline is absolute. Late filing = no lien. Your daily reports serve as the definitive record of when work was performed, eliminating disputes about the last-furnishing date that GCs and owners commonly exploit.

Enforcement Period

Once filed, your lien is valid for 1 year. If payment is not received, you must initiate foreclosure proceedings within this window. Without contemporaneous daily documentation, proving your case in court becomes your word against theirs.
