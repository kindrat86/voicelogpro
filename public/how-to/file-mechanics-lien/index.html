How to File a Mechanics Lien

Guide to filing a mechanics lien to secure payment for construction work.

A mechanics lien is one of the most powerful tools for getting paid in construction. But deadlines are strict and requirements vary by state.

Step 1: Send a Preliminary Notice

Many states require a preliminary notice (also called a Notice to Owner) before you can file a lien. Check your state's requirements immediately upon starting work.

Step 2: Track the Deadline

Lien deadlines range from 30 days to 12 months after last work, depending on the state. Missing the deadline means losing your lien rights permanently.

Step 3: File the Lien

File the lien with the county recorder where the property is located. Include the property description, amount owed, and labor/materials provided.

Step 4: Enforce If Necessary

If payment is still not made, you may need to foreclose on the lien through a lawsuit. Most liens are resolved before this stage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do I have to file a mechanics lien?

It varies by state. California: 90 days after completion. Texas: 15 days after last work for monthly notices. Florida: 90 days. Check your specific state requirements immediately.
